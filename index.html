The main purpose of this webpage is to give a brief description of the Tupinambis Merianae, commonly known as the Tegu Lizard, and to provide information on where you can surrender your unwanted pet Tegu if necessary.


Tegu Lizards are not part of the natural ecosystem of Florida. They are native to South America, specifically to Brazil, Paraguay, Uruguay, and Argentina. Tegus have become locally established in Manatee, Hillsborough, and Miami-Dade Counties due to releases or escapes of pets. Tegus are an exotic invasive species which reproduces quickly and eat a wide variety of food items, including small animals and eggs of many wildlife species.

It is imperative that if you no longer want or can no longer care for your pet Tegu, that you take the responsible measures to surrender it. Please do not release your pet Tegu out into the wild. You can surrender your pet tegu with no penalties and no questions asked at most reptile shelters or at any Amnesty Day Event hosted by the FWC. Should you see a Tegu in the wild, do not try to capture, please report the sighting to IveGot1 for proper removal.
